# OpenGitBase Commercial License

This document describes when a commercial license is required and what it
grants. It supplements the [LICENSE](LICENSE). In case of conflict, the
written Commercial License agreement controls.

## When a commercial license is required

You must obtain a Commercial License before using OpenGitBase in production if:

1. **Large organization** — Your organization has annual recurring revenue
   (ARR) of **$1,000,000 US Dollars or more**, or **more than 300 employees**, and you
   use OpenGitBase for internal production workloads (not covered by the
   Open-Source Project exemption).

2. **Third-party access** — You provide access to OpenGitBase, or to
   repositories or services operated through it, to anyone **outside your
   organization**. This includes managed hosting, SaaS, PaaS, resale, and
   similar offerings — **regardless of your organization's size or revenue**.

## Who does not need a commercial license

Free production use is permitted under the [LICENSE](LICENSE) when:

- Your organization has **ARR below $1M (US Dollar)** and **300 or fewer employees**, and
  use is **internal only** (no third-party access), or
- Use is solely for an **OSI-licensed open-source project**, regardless of
  sponsor size.

See [docs/licensing.md](docs/licensing.md) for worked examples.

## What a commercial license grants

A Commercial License, once agreed in writing, typically grants:

- Production use beyond the free-tier limits in the LICENSE
- Production use with third-party access (hosting providers)
- Trademark use as specified in the agreement (see [TRADEMARK.md](TRADEMARK.md))
- Support and pricing terms as agreed

## How to obtain a license

Commercial licensing is not yet available for purchase. When ready, contact
information will be published here.

Until then, if your use requires a commercial license, contact the project
maintainers before deploying to production.

## Enforcement

OpenGitBase uses an **honor system**: there is no license key,
phone-home, or technical enforcement in the software. You are responsible for
determining whether your use requires a commercial license and for obtaining
one when required.

## Disclaimer

This document is a summary for convenience. The [LICENSE](LICENSE) and any
signed Commercial License agreement are the binding terms. Have legal counsel
review before relying on these terms for your organization.
