# OpenGitBase Trademark Policy

The **OpenGitBase** name, logo, and related branding are trademarks of the
OpenGitBase project and its copyright holders. This policy explains permitted
and prohibited uses. It supplements the [LICENSE](LICENSE).

## Permitted uses

You may use the OpenGitBase name in the following ways **without** a separate
trademark license, provided your use of the software complies with the
[LICENSE](LICENSE):

- **Attribution** — Statements such as "powered by OpenGitBase" or "built on
  OpenGitBase" on documentation, about pages, or admin UIs for deployments
  that comply with the LICENSE.

- **Factual reference** — Describing compatibility, integration, or
  relationship with OpenGitBase in truthful, non-misleading terms (e.g.
  "migration guide from OpenGitBase").

- **Nominative use** — Referring to OpenGitBase by name when necessary to
  identify the product, without implying endorsement or affiliation.

## Prohibited uses without approval

You may **not** use OpenGitBase trademarks without written permission or a
Commercial License that includes trademark rights:

- In the **name** of a competing product, hosted service, or public fork
  (e.g. "OpenGitBase Cloud by Acme", "AcmeGitBase").

- In a way that **implies endorsement, sponsorship, or affiliation** with the
  OpenGitBase project when none exists.

- On **merchandise** or marketing materials for a product or service that does
  not comply with the LICENSE.

- To **rebrand** a public fork or derivative so it appears to be an official
  OpenGitBase product or release.

## Forks and derivatives

If you publish a **public fork** or derivative of the source code:

- You **must rename** the product so it is not confusingly similar to
  OpenGitBase (e.g. do not use "OpenGitBase" or confusingly similar spellings
  as your product name).

- You **must not** use OpenGitBase logos or visual identity for your fork.

- You may state the project is "based on OpenGitBase" with appropriate
  attribution.

This mirrors common open-core practice (e.g. Mattermost trademark standards):
code may be forked under the LICENSE, but the brand may not.
